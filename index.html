Chadwick Boseman

The image featured here of Chadwick Boseman was taken days before the release of Black Panther in theaters. This is the image of a man fighting a private battle with colon cancer while upholding the mantle of many black icons with compassion, grace, and dignity. In so doing, he helped pave the way for his people to follow.

Durring his private battle with colon cancer, Chadwick visited and inspired many children fighting their own battles with cancer, keeping in touch with many of them. Chadwick was diagnosed with cancer in 2016. On Friday, August 28, 2020, he passed away. May his legacy light the way for years to come. Rest in Power.

A Time Line of Chadwick's Life:

  • 1976 - Chadwick Boseman was born in Anderson, South Carolina to Carolyn and Leroy Boseman.
  • 1993 - Chadwick wrote his first play, Crossroads, which he staged at school after a classmate was shote and killed.
  • 1995 - Chadwick graduated from T.L. High School where he played in the basketball team.
  • 2000 - Chadwick graduated from Howard University in Washington D.C. with a Bachelor in Fine Arts in Directing.
  • 2003 - Chadwick was cast in his first television role, and episode of Third Watch.
  • 2008 - Boseman played a recurring rolke on the television series Lincoln Heights and appearned in his first feature film, The Express: The Ernie Davis Story, as running back Floyd Little.
  • 2010 - Boseman landed his first regulare role in the television series Persons Unknown.
  • 2013 - Chadwick recieved what would be his breakthrough role in the film 42, in which he portrayed baseball pioneer and star Jackie Robinson.
  • 2016 - Boseman starred as Thoth, an Egyptian deity, in Gods of Egypt, as one of the few actors of color featured in the film. This fact motivated Boseman to accept the role to ensure one of the film's deities would be of african decent.
  • 2016 - Boseman made his first appearance as T'challa (Black Panther) in Captain America: Civil War, after which he would go on to reprise his role as T'challa in Black Panther (2018), Avengers: Infinity War (2018), and Avenger's Endgame (2019).
  • 2016 - Boseman was diagnosed with stage III colon cancer, which would eventually progress to stage IV before 2020.
  • 2019 - Boseman starred in 21 Bridges, an American action thriller.
  • 2020 - Boseman died in his home as a result of complications related to colon cancer, with his wife and family by his side.
